WHO WE ARE

Inspired by the stories of self and that of their patients and communities, SocMed Global Uganda is a collective of like minded health professionals who live, practice, and accompany patients and communities through their illness journey across all corners of Uganda.

Challenged by the incessant disparities in health outcomes across population groups and geographies, we strive to reimagine equitable approaches to healthcare delivery, education, and research that centers communities experiencing and living the effects of social, economic, cultural, geographical, and political disenfranchisement. .

Our mission is to promote healthy equity through practice, education, research, and empowerment.

Service to community

In 2021, we partnered with EqualHealth to support the Amuru village health team (VHT) and Paimol community during the COVID-19 pandemic. Through this partnership, we provided over 5000 disposable hand gloves, 200 liters of hand sanitizer, and 700 bars of soap to aid safe practice in communities in the thick of the pandemic.

OUR STORY

Drawing on the principles of personal, praxis, and partnership, SocMed Global Uganda adopts a local glocal approach. We live and function in Uganda but are conscious of the complex interplay of the myriad global economic and political forces that affect the lives of our communities locally.
In 2018, following the annual SMC conference in the Navajo nation, New Mexico, USA a power house of emerging leaders in health equity Ainembabazi Provia, Alex Olirus Owilli, Aidah Nanvuma, Aboda Noah, Stella Namuganza, Brian Turigye, and Angella Namwatse co-founded a Uganda local chapter of the Social Medicine Consortium (SMC) network.
In the same year, under the leadership of Ainembabazi Provia, we implemented a local campaign to build community consciousness on the impact of racism and how it manifests and informs systems and behaviors in Uganda. We have grown through these activities and have expanded to support the delivery of the social medicine course offered by EqualHealth.
