WHO WE ARE

Inspired by the stories of self and that of their patients and communities, SocMed Global Uganda is a collective of like minded health professionals who live, practice, and accompany patients and communities through their illness journey across all corners of Uganda.

Challenged by the incessant disparities in health outcomes across population groups and geographies, we strive to reimagine equitable approaches to healthcare delivery, education and research that centers communities experiencing and living the effects of social, economic, cultural, geographical, and political disenfranchisement.

Our Vision

A Uganda free of health inequities

Our Mission

To promote health equity in Uganda, through practice, education, research, and empowerment

Our Slogan

SocMed Global Uganda "Health Equity for all"




SOCMED GLOBAL UGANDA

OUR STORY

SocMed Global Uganda was born from a shared conviction that health cannot be separated from the social, economic, and structural realities that shape people's lives. Grounded in the principles of persona, praxis, and partnership, we take a glocal approach rooted in Uganda while remaining deeply aware of the global forces that influence health and equity within our communities.

In 2018, following the annual Social Medicine Consortium conference in Navajo Nation, New Mexico, a group of emerging leaders in health equity; Provia Ainembabazi, Alex Olirus Owilli, Aidah Nanvuma, Noah Aboda, Stella Namuganza, Brian Turigye and Angella Namwatse came together to establish the Uganda Chapter of the Social Medicine Consortium.

Today, SocMed Global Uganda operates autonomously, registered as a company limited by guarantee in 2023, continuing to uphold the philosophy and community-centered approach that shaped its origins.

OUR IMPACT

Challenging Structural Inequities in Health

Through global South coalition building, partnerships, and interdisciplinary organizing, we connect local lived experiences to global advocacy efforts aimed at advancing justice and dismantling systemic inequities in health and governance.
