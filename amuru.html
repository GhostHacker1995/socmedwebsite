Project Name
CareConnect Amuru

Summary
The CareConnect Amuru project aims to enhance health equity in rural Uganda by addressing transportation barriers between Village Health team members (VHT) / community healthcare workers and local populations. We will provide bicycles to enable the community healthcare workers to reach community members more efficiently, facilitating timely healthcare delivery and referrals to nearest healthcare facilities. To ensure sustainability, we shall support a VHT run bicycle maintenance workshop that will generate income while also serving as a hub for community health education and outreach.

Project vision
An efficient health service delivery system in Amuru- Uganda.

Organising Statement

We are organising the SocMed Global Uganda and Amuru Village Health Team association to achieve universal health care coverage in Uganda. We shall achieve this by improving the mobility of village health team members / Community health care workers to improve service delivery in communities by lobbying for bikes for VHTs. We shall employ tactics of fundraising/lobbying and documentary on the journey of VHTs to document and showcase challenges that VHTs encounter on a daily basis while executing their duties.

Strategic Objectives and Methods

  • To document the journey of VHTs:
    A documentary to show the journey the VHTs make as they deliver their services in the community will be captured on video-audio. The video recording will be edited and the final and approved version uploaded on the website and YouTube channels for partner organisations for publicity. The publicity from this documentary will boost the fundraising campaign.
  • To lobby/fundraise for funds to support bikes repair and purchase:
    A campaign to raise 100,000 USD will be launched. Various channels including, the social medicine student network, Go Fund me, web donations through partner organisations and one on one engagement with potential donors seeking financial support, grant applications will be employed.
  • To repair the broken repairable bikes and purchase the bikes for VHTs:
    Following the lobbying of funds, we shall repair repairable bikes and purchase new bikes to meet the current demand based on the availability of funds.
  • To monitor and evaluate the project’s impact:
    The impact will be measured by both output and outcome indicators. These will include measures such as reduction in time for service delivery, job satisfaction, quality of life/stress for VHTs, improved referral to health facilities using surveys and in-depth interviews.
