ADVOCACY & SOCIAL JUSTICE

Advancing Health Equity Through Critical Consciousness and Collective Action

We are building a transformative advocacy platform that centers decolonizing approaches to health equity, where communities and practitioners in the Global South actively shape health systems, policies, and narratives grounded in lived realities and justice.

Methodology

Our Approach:
Four Interconnected Pillars

Our work is grounded in four interconnected pillars that guide how we think, engage, and act — building a platform for transformative and sustained advocacy for health equity.

Education & Critical Consciousness

We strengthen political education processes that deepen critical awareness among communities, health practitioners, and partners — enabling them to analyze, question, and respond to the structural and historical drivers of health inequities.

Capacity Strengthening

We enhance the leadership, technical, and organizational capacities of community groups and health actors to effectively engage in advocacy, influence decision-making, and implement locally driven solutions to health inequities.

Narrative Change & Knowledge Production

We build and sustain community-informed spaces for knowledge generation and exchange that elevate lived experiences and perspectives, challenging dominant narratives and reshaping how health and equity are understood.

Policy & Systems Advocacy

We explore partnerships with local, regional, national, and global institutions to foster policy and systems change — advancing more equitable, accountable, and community-centered health systems.

Health inequities are produced and sustained by systems, histories, and power structures — not isolated factors.

In Action

Debates & Dialogues for Structural Change

We create spaces for critical dialogue that bring together students, academics, and communities to examine how systems shape health and society. Through structured debates and academic exchanges, we foster deeper understanding of structural determinants and strengthen collective thinking on justice and equity.

March 2026

Interschool Debates

In March 2026, we convened a powerful interschool debating session bringing together secondary school students to critically engage with the theme "Racial Capitalism and Its Footprint."

The debate created a rare space for young learners to explore how historical and contemporary systems of racialized global and economic powers continue to shape inequality, opportunity, and health outcomes. Through argument, reflection, and peer exchange, students developed early critical thinking skills around justice, systems, and social change.

June 2024

Grand Rounds & Awareness — Gulu University

We hosted a multidisciplinary grand round at Gulu University that focused on unpacking racial capitalism as a complex and often misunderstood determinant of health.

The session brought together students, clinicians, and leaders to explore how global economic and political systems influence health inequalities — examining the intersections between racism, capitalism, and structural violence, and how these forces manifest within health systems and everyday lived experiences.

Through advocacy, we are strengthening the ability of communities and practitioners to understand these systems and actively participate in transforming them. Health equity is not a gift — it is won through sustained collective action.

Results

Programme Impact Highlights

Through advocacy, we are working toward a future where communities lead in shaping the systems that affect their health and well-being.

Building critical consciousness on structural determinants of health among students, clinicians, and community members

Strengthening capacity among communities and health actors to engage meaningfully in advocacy

Advancing alternative narratives rooted in lived experience and Global South perspectives

Engaging institutions in structured dialogue on health equity, justice, and systems transformation

Why This Matters

Transforming health systems requires more than awareness — it requires collective power, sustained dialogue, and intentional action.

By creating spaces where communities analyze systems, produce knowledge, and demand change, we are building the conditions for genuinely equitable health outcomes — locally and globally.
