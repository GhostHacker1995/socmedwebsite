Social Medicine education

The social medicine program partners with Equal Health in delivery of the social medicine curriculum in Uganda annually through attending curriculum review meetings, curriculum development, support the application and selection process and delivery of the course in Uganda and on transnational sessions with students from Haiti and USA.
It will also advocate for incorporation of a social medicine curriculum for higher education of learning in Uganda. This is done through holding consultative meetings with heads of education and curriculum development in institutions of higher learning, and support higher institutions of learning with formulation of the social medicine course

Our mission is to promote healthy equity through practice, education, research, and empowerment.
OUR PROGRAMS

Service to community

In 2021, we partnered with EqualHealth to support the Amuru village health team (VHT) and Paimol community during the COVID-19 pandemic. Through this partnership, we provided over 5000 disposable hand gloves, 200 liters of hand sanitizer, and 700 bars of soap to aid safe practice in communities in the thick of the pandemic.
In 2022, we again partnered with EqualHealth to respond to the hunger epidemic that plugged Karamoja region. At the time, more than 20 people were dying daily due to lack of food. We supplied over 7000 kilograms of beans and posho that impacted the lives of more than 700 households in the affected communities of Abim district. The impact of climate change, emerging and reimaging epidemics, preventable injuries, and forced emigration continue to put pressure on the stretched systems of demanding knee jack responses. The critical question to policy makers, researchers, practitioners, and advocates alike is how do create communities that are resilient to the impact of external and internal system stressors?” We know it is not an easy task to get there, but the change that we want starts with us and must begin now. Campaign Against Racism ( CAR)

The Campaign Against Racism program is supported through a partnership with EqualHealth and the organization acts as the Uganda Campaign Against Racism Chapter and works with the Equal Health CAR program and its strategic direction is informed by the Equal Health CAR program strategic priorities. Our current strategic goal is to dismantle racial capitalism through a shared global struggle that will center the local experience of the lived impact of racial capitalistic nature in global systems of governance.
Through adopting the global south coalition for vaccine strategy, building partnerships and interdisciplinary organizing, we are buiding power with tactics that take these local injustices to a global organizing strategy that dismantles the operationalization of racial capitalism within and beyond the health context.

Trustworthy AI uganda

The Trustworthy AI uganda Lab is affiliated with the Z-inspection® initiative. Z-Inspection® is a holistic process for evaluating the trustworthiness of AI-based technologies at different stages of the AI lifecycle. In particular, it focuses on identifying and discussing ethical issues and tensions through the development of socio-technical scenarios. The process has been published in the IEEE Transactions on Technology and Society. Z-Inspection® is distributed under the terms of the Creative Commons License (Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike CC BY-NC-SA). Z-Inspection® is listed in the new OECD Catalogue of AI Tools & Metrics.
