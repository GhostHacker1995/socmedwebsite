ABOUT THE PROGRAMME

Global Health in Local Contexts

The course brings together health professional students, residents, graduates, and practitioners from Uganda, Haiti, and Minnesota (USA) into a shared learning space — equipping them with the knowledge, skills, and critical perspective to understand and address the social determinants of health.

Running from September to December 2026, the programme explores how social and structural determinants shape health outcomes while equipping participants with the skills to lead meaningful change. Through this model, we are building a global community of health professionals grounded in equity, collaboration, and action.

This is not a traditional classroom experience. It is an immersive, interdisciplinary learning journey that connects theory to real-world practice. Participants engage through community experiences, reflective dialogue, creative expression, and cross-cultural exchange — exploring how biological, social, economic, and political forces intersect to shape health outcomes.

OUR METHODOLOGY

Our Approach: The 3Ps Model

Praxis

We believe meaningful learning happens through the continuous interaction between reflection and action — enabling participants to critically analyze health systems and respond effectively to the communities they serve.

Personal

We center lived experience. Participants engage in critical self-reflection on identity, power, privilege, and their role in advancing health equity — understanding how their own stories connect to the systems they work within.

Partnership

We foster collaboration across diverse backgrounds, disciplines, and geographies — building a community that drives innovative and just solutions to health challenges at local and global scales.

LEARNING OUTCOMES

What Participants Gain

Participants enhance their ability to engage deeply with the structural forces shaping health — and emerge equipped to drive change in clinical, research, policy, and community settings.

  • Analyze the social determinants of health across local and global contexts
  • Apply social medicine frameworks to real-world health challenges
  • Engage in critical self-reflection on power, identity, and leadership
  • Work effectively within diverse, inter-professional teams and communities
  • Advocate for policies and systems that advance health equity
  • Communicate across disciplines and cultural contexts

Who Can Apply

The programme is open to health professional students in higher institutions who are passionate about advancing equitable healthcare systems and engaging in transformative global health work.

We welcome applicants from medicine, nursing, pharmacy, public health, social work, and related disciplines who are committed to equity, justice, and community-centered care.

THE BIGGER PICTURE

Why This Matters

Health inequities persist not only because of limited resources, but because health systems often fail to address the social and structural drivers of disease. Poverty, geography, gender, race, and political exclusion all shape who gets sick — and who gets care.

By training health professionals to understand and respond to these realities, the programme contributes to stronger, more equitable health systems both locally and globally. Our work is part of a shared global effort to reimagine healthcare — one that requires continuous learning, collaboration, and action.

We are cultivating a growing network of health professionals who are applying social medicine principles across clinical care, public health, research, leadership, advocacy, and community engagement.
